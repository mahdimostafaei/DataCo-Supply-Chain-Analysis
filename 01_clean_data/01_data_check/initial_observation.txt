3. KPI & Metric Definitions

Net Revenue
Business Meaning:
Revenue after discounts.
Calculation:
SUM(Order Item Total)
Source Column:
Order Item Total

Total Units Sold
Business Meaning:
Total quantity sold.
Calculation:
SUM(Order Item Quantity)
Source Column:
Order Item Quantity

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
Business Meaning:
Average revenue generated per customer.
Calculation
Revenue/Distinct Customer Count

Revenue Growth (YoY)
Business Meaning:
Revenue growth compared with the previous year.
Calculation:
(Current Year Revenue − Previous Year Revenue)/Previous Year Revenue

Customer Growth (YoY)
Business Meaning:
Growth in unique customers compared with the previous year.
Calculation:
(Current Year Customers − Previous Year Customers)/Previous Year Customers

Repurchase Rate
Business Meaning:
Percentage of customers who purchased more than once.
Calculation:
Customers With More Than One Order/Total Customers

Retention Rate
Business Meaning:
Percentage of customers who return and purchase again.
Calculation:
Returning Customers/Total Customers

Churn Rate
Business Meaning:
Customers active in a previous period but not active in the current period.
Calculation:
Lost Customers/Previous Period Customers

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
Business Meaning:
Average revenue generated by a customer over their purchasing lifetime.
Calculation:
Total Customer Revenue/Customer Count

Average Scheduled Delivery Time
Calculation:
AVG(Days for shipment (scheduled))
Average Actual Delivery Time
Calculation:
AVG(Days for shipment (real))

On-Time Delivery Rate
Business Meaning:
Percentage of deliveries completed on time.
Calculation:
(Late Delivery Risk(flag column) == 0)/Total Deliveries

Average Order Value (AOV)
Business Meaning:
Average revenue generated per transaction.
Calculation:
Net Revenue/Transaction Count

Revenue Concentration
Business Meaning:
Measure the degree to which revenue is generated by a small subset of products.
Calculation Approach:
Calculate revenue by product.
Rank products by revenue descending.
Calculate cumulative revenue percentage.
Determine what percentage of products generates:
50% of revenue
80% of revenue
90% of revenue
